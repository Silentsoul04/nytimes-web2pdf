<div class="l-container">

<div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--sourced-paragraph">

(CNN)Doctored videos of [Tuesday's devastating explosion in
Beirut](https://edition.cnn.com/2020/08/05/europe/lebanon-russian-ship-blast-intl/index.html)
are already circulating on every major social media platform.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

In videos originally shot by both CNN and eyewitnesses in the Lebanese
capital, footage of a rising smoke plume at Beirut Port was inverted and
made to look like a "negative." In addition, a missile-like object was
superimposed on the video.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

Some of the manipulated footage was taken from the Facebook page of
Beirut-based, CNN Arabic social media producer [Mehsen
Mekhtfe](https://twitter.com/MehsenMekhtfe?s=20). He had captured the
explosion while walking near the port -- something that he does
frequently during that time of day.

</div>

<div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

<div data-ad-id="ad_rect_atf_02" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default">

<div id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default">

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"Many people reached out to me to tell me that it's fake," Mekhtfe told
CNN, referring to the fake missile video. "But it's my video and I have
the original and it doesn't show that. When people ask me about it, I
tell them, the doctored one is not true."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"I can emphasize that I didn't see any missile and didn't hear any jet
or drone above me," he added.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__read-more">

<div class="read-more-gradient">

</div>

<div class="read-more-button" data-zjs="click" data-zjs-campaign="readmore-btn-mobile">

<div id="js-body-read-more" class="read-more-link">

Read More

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__read-all">

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

CNN Arabic's team in Dubai was first to spot the footage, and
immediately recognized it had been doctored.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The manipulated footage can be found on every major social media
platform: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, YouTube and TikTok.

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Some of the videos on Facebook had a "false information" warning,
including one that had been shared more than 1,500 times. Others did not
bear that warning, including one with over 8,400 views.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

CNN contacted the social media companies for comment on the doctored
videos but has received a response from TikTok, Twitter and YouTube.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"As soon as we became aware of this video, it was removed for violating
our policy on misleading content," a TikTok spokesperson told CNN.
"Prior to removal, the video had already been automatically flagged by
our system, limiting its reach on our platform. Our hearts go out to the
people of Beirut during this difficult time."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"We have removed the video for violating our Community Guidelines and
re-uploads of the original clip if they contain segments that we deem to
be violative of YouTube's Community Guidelines," Farshad Shadloo,
YouTube's head of policy communications, told CNN in an email.

</div>

<div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

<div data-ad-id="ad_rect_btf_01" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default">

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"The video in question is marked as synthetic and manipulated media," a
Twitter spokesperson told CNN. That means anywhere the video shows up,
it should bear a label showing it has been doctored.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

When CNN contacted the individual credited with creating the doctored
video for comment, the person responded, "Someone or somebody hated me
so much to put my email on a fake video."

</div>
