<div class="l-container">

<div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--sourced-paragraph">

(CNN)Authorities in the Chinese region of Inner Mongolia have sealed off
a village after a resident there died from [bubonic
plague](https://www.cnn.com/2019/05/08/health/bubonic-plague-explainer-trnd/index.html),
a centuries-old disease responsible for the most deadly pandemic in
human history.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

The death was reported to health authorities in Baotou city on Sunday
and the victim was confirmed to be a bubonic plague patient on Thursday,
the Baotou Municipal Health Commission said in a
[statement](http://wjw.baotou.gov.cn/c/2020-08-06/1418104.html) on its
website.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

The patient died of circulatory system failure, according to the
statement. It did not mention how the patient had caught the plague.

</div>

<div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

<div data-ad-id="ad_rect_atf_02" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default">

<div id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default">

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

To curb the spread of the disease, authorities sealed off Suji Xincun
village, where the dead patient lived, and ordered daily disinfection of
homes. All villagers have so far tested negative for the disease, the
statement said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Nine close contacts and 26 secondary contacts of the patient have been
quarantined and tested negative, the commission said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__read-more">

<div class="read-more-gradient">

</div>

<div class="read-more-button" data-zjs="click" data-zjs-campaign="readmore-btn-mobile">

<div id="js-body-read-more" class="read-more-link">

Read More

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__read-all">

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Damao Banner, the district where the village is located, has been put on
Level 3 alert for plague prevention, the second lowest in a four-level
system, until the end of the year.

</div>

<div class="el__embedded el__embedded--standard">

<div class="el__storyelement--standard el__article--embed">

<div class="el__article--teaseimage">

<div class="img__preloader">

</div>

![Chinese authorities confirm case of bubonic plague in Inner

</div>

<div class="media__caption el__storyelement__title">

<span class="el__storyelement__header">[Chinese authorities confirm case
of bubonic plague in Inner
Mongolia](/2020/07/06/asia/china-mongolia-bubonic-plague-intl-hnk-scli-scn/index.html)</span>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

This is the second case -- and first death -- of bubonic plague China
has confirmed this year. [The previous
case](https://www.cnn.com/2020/07/06/asia/china-mongolia-bubonic-plague-intl-hnk-scli-scn/index.html)
was discovered in July in Bayannur, another city in Inner Mongolia,
leading to the issuing of another Level 3 alert and the closure of
several tourist spots.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Plague, caused by bacteria and transmitted through flea bites and
infected animals, killed an estimated 50 million people in Europe during
the Black Death pandemic in the Middle Ages.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Bubonic plague, which is one of plague's three forms, causes painful,
swollen lymph nodes, as well as fever, chills, and coughing.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The advent of antibiotics, which can treat most infections if they are
caught early enough, has helped to contain plague outbreaks, preventing
the type of rapid spread witnessed in Europe in the Middle Ages.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

But it has not been eliminated it entirely -- and it has made a recent
comeback, leading the World Health Organization (WHO) to categorize it
as a re-emerging disease.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

### Common recurrence

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Anywhere from 1,000 to 2,000 people get the plague every year, according
to the WHO. But that total is likely too modest an estimate, since it
doesn't account for unreported cases.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

According to 2016 data, the possibility of plague exists on almost every
continent, especially the western United States, parts of Brazil,
scattered areas in southeast Africa and large swaths of China, India and
the Middle East.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

[In the
US](https://www.cnn.com/2019/08/17/us/bubonic-plague-colorado/index.html),
there have been anywhere from a few to a few dozen cases of plague every
year, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. In
2015, two people in Colorado died from the plague, and the year before
there were eight reported cases in the state.

</div>

<div class="el__storyelement--standard el__article--embed">

<span class="el__storyelement__header">[Why is bubonic plague still a
thing?](/2019/05/08/health/bubonic-plague-explainer-trnd/index.html)
</span>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

In China, 31 cases of plague were reported between 2009 and 2019,
including 12 deaths, according to data released by the National Health
Commission.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

On Thursday, Baotou authorities warned of a risk of "a human plague
epidemic spreading in the city," and urged the public to take extra
precautions and seek immediate medical attention if they develop
symptoms of fever or coughing.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

They also urged people to reduce contact with wild animals while
traveling and avoid hunting, skinning or eating animals that could cause
infection.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Last month, two cases of bubonic plague were confirmed in Mongolia --
brothers who had both eaten marmot meat, according to China's state-run
news agency Xinhua. In May 2019, another couple in Mongolia died from
the plague after eating the raw kidney of a marmot, thought to be a folk
remedy for good health.

</div>

<div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

<div data-ad-id="ad_rect_btf_01" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default">

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Marmots a type of large ground squirrel that is eaten in some parts of
China and the neighboring country Mongolia, and which have historically
caused plague outbreaks in the region.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The marmot is believed to have caused the 1911 pneumonic plague
epidemic, which killed about 63,000 people in northeast China. It was
hunted for its fur, which soared in popularity among international
traders. The diseased fur products were traded and transported around
the country -- infecting thousands along the way.
