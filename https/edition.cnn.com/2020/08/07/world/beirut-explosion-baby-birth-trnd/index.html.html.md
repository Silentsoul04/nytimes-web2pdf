<div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--sourced-paragraph">

(CNN)If you ask Edmond Khnaisser to summarize the birth of his son, he'd
tell you it was an adrenaline rush. And not because baby George is his
firstborn, but because his son was born just moments after the massive
Beirut explosion ripped through his wife's hospital room.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

Edmond and his wife, Emma, checked into Saint George Hospital on Tuesday
morning fully prepared for the birth of their son later that day. With
Emma in the pre-labor room, Edmond decided to [take a
video](https://www.facebook.com/edmondkhnaisser/posts/10159118501194131?__cft__%5B0%5D=AZWFORcA44HsMLJcDFgxM6e_wXiMv535psv0A0gwKN0amp9R5bnFzRvjKWed8BbcT4Z6mVqouBpP45RtEsRX71OrOMlL3hLMxZsKsu0TXzfPX0PinCjZbGd6_FuFg7LkIxU&__tn__=%2CO%2CP-R)
of their last moments as a family of two. While recoding, Edmond enters
the doorway of Emma's room and the explosion can be heard, and broken
glass from the windows falls to the ground.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

"Everything was shattered around," Edmond said. "I went and got
everything off my wife, helped a nurse get off the floor right in front
of her bed and then I pulled my wife's bed to a safe location."

</div>

<div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

<div data-ad-id="ad_rect_atf_02" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default">

<div id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default">

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Edmond said he wheeled Emma's bed to a room that appeared to be
untouched by the explosion until all the nurses and doctors, who were
injured themselves, could attend to her.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"They stitched up each other, they were very professional and then
started the labor as if nothing had occurred," he said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__read-more">

<div class="read-more-gradient">

</div>

<div class="read-more-button" data-zjs="click" data-zjs-campaign="readmore-btn-mobile">

<div id="js-body-read-more" class="read-more-link">

Read More

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__read-all">

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The main operation room, medications and instruments were completely
destroyed, Edmond said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"My wife had to give birth without any medicine," he said. "At the same
time there was no lighting, so doctors and nurses were shining light on
my wife using cellphones."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

### Straddling two spaces

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Both Emma and Edmond's parents had been waiting on the other side of the
hospital, so once his wife was in the hands of doctors and safe, he ran
to find their family.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"It was actually a maze, Edmond said. "Everything was totally
destroyed."

</div>

<div class="el__embedded el__embedded--fullwidth">

<div class="el__image--fullwidth js__image--fullwidth">

<div>

![baby in crib
](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhEAAJAJEAAAAAAP///////wAAACH5BAEAAAIALAAAAAAQAAkAAAIKlI+py+0Po5yUFQA7)

<div class="img__preloader">

</div>

![baby in crib
](//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200807151621-03-beirut-baby-birth-explosion-trnd-restricted-large-169.jpg)

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"They put my wife on a plastic chair, took her down to the parking lot
and we had to walk around 600 meters with my wife in new stitches and a
10 minute year old baby just so we could get a car to get us to
somewhere safe," he said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Edmond's mom suffered a lung injury because of broken limbs but he said
she's stable, recovering and out of the ICU.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"When a person is in such a situation, you don't think about what will
happen to you," he said. "When you see someone so weak in front of you,
you just act."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

And even after the couple made their way outside Saint George's and
traveled 5 miles east to a neighboring one, Hôpital Aboujaoudé, Edmond
said they were still so shocked at the impact the explosion had on their
surroundings.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"We didn't know that it was that big and this much people were hurt," he
said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Edmond is calling doctors and Emma superheroes.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"They (doctors) could have just left," he said. "They could be scared
and leave but none of them left."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Since baby George's birth, the Saint George's doctors have kept in
contact with the couple every day.

</div>

<div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show">

<div data-ad-id="ad_rect_btf_01" data-ad-position="mobile" data-ad-refresh="default">

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"It was just like a family on that floor," he said. "Like sisters and
brothers in that hospital."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Edmond and Emma were finally able to take baby George home on Friday
evening and he said he's just thankful his family is safe and healthy.

</div>
