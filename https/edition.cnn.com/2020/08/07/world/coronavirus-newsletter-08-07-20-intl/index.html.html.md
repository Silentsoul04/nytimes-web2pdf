<div class="el__leafmedia el__leafmedia--sourced-paragraph">

(CNN)More than [19 million people around the
world](https://cnn.com/interactive/2020/health/coronavirus-us-maps-and-cases/)
have now been infected with the coronavirus, and 715,024 have died. The
number of new daily cases has topped 200,000 -- a level not seen before
July -- for 23 days in row.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">

In India, the number of infections [surpassed 2 million
today](https://cnn.com/2020/08/07/asia/india-coronavirus-two-million-intl-hnk/index.html).
The speed at which the virus has spread around the country of 1.3
billion people is stunning. India reported its first case on January 30.
It took almost six months to reach 1 million cases, then only three more
weeks to add another million.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Meanwhile, there are now 1 million reported cases in Africa -- more than
half of them in South Africa -- although the World Health Organization
said the actual number of infections in the region might be much higher
because [testing remains a
challenge](https://cnn.com/world/live-news/coronavirus-pandemic-08-07-20-intl/h_a189fc2d94d7d9b51b92fcdf1bdf664e).

</div>

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

And Brazil, the world's second worst-hit country, is fast approaching 3
million cases and 100,000 deaths.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

But it is the United States that is seeing the [highest numbers of
infections and
deaths](http://www.cnn.com/2020/08/07/health/us-coronavirus-friday/index.html).
More than 4.8 million Americans have been infected -- approximately one
in 75 -- and more than 160,000 have died.

</div>

<div class="read-more-gradient">

<div class="zn-body__read-all">

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The numbers could get much worse. Several US states are seeing an
increase in the percentage of tests coming back positive, and Dr.
Anthony Fauci said this was a predictor of trouble ahead. "It's a clear
indication that you are getting an uptick in cases, which inevitably, as
we've seen in the southern states, leads to surges, and then you get
hospitalizations and then you get deaths," [Fauci
told](https://cnn.com/videos/health/2020/08/06/dr-anthony-fauci-birx-warning-trouble-ahead-newday-vpx.cnn)
CNN yesterday.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention is now projecting more
than 181,000 coronavirus deaths in the United States by August 29. That
means the CDC expects almost 1,000 Americans to die on average every day
in the next three weeks.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Looking further ahead, the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation
at the University of Washington's School of Medicine foresees nearly
[300,000 US
deaths](http://www.cnn.com/2020/08/06/health/us-coronavirus-thursday/index.html)
by December 1. It doesn't have to be that way. The institute said 70,000
lives can be saved between now and December, if only more people started
wearing masks.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"Starting today, if 95% of the people in the US were to wear masks when
leaving their homes, that total number would decrease to 228,271 deaths,
a drop of 49%," said IHME Director Dr. Christopher Murray.

</div>

### **YOU ASKED. WE ANSWERED**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

**Q: Should I get a mask with a vent?**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

**A:** No. The CDC does not recommend people use masks with valves or
vents during the pandemic. The one-way valves may provide more comfort,
since the valves allow air to escape from the mask and keep the face
cooler, but they also allow the virus to escape.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The CDC encourages people to wear masks so they don't infect others.
Covid-19 spreads mainly through respiratory droplets when a person
talks, sneezes, sings or coughs. Masks can help stop those respiratory
droplets.

</div>

### **WHAT'S IMPORTANT TODAY**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

**It's ok not to be ok**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

"The idea that what this country is going through shouldn't have any
effect on us—that we all should just feel OK all the time—that just
doesn't feel real to me. So I hope you all are allowing yourselves to
feel whatever it is you're feeling."

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The [words of former First Lady Michelle
Obama](https://cnn.com/2020/08/06/us/michelle-obama-coronavirus-depression-trnd/index.html)
rang true to many across America and around the world. Obama said she
was suffering from a "low-grade depression" caused by worries about the
pandemic, race relations in the US and the political strife surrounding
it all.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

A new report from the Commonwealth Fund into the impact of the pandemic
on mental health found that many adults reported experiencing stress,
anxiety, or great sadness that was difficult to cope with alone since
the outbreak started. But while there were people who were suffering in
every one of the 10 developed countries the researchers looked at, the
report found that [Americans were impacted much
more](https://cnn.com/2020/08/06/health/us-coronavirus-mental-health-problems-wellness/index.html):
33% of Americans have said they felt anxious, stressed or sad. The
countries with the next highest levels were Canada and the UK, both of
which reported 26% of people feeling anxious or stressed.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The country with the lowest levels of stress, anxiety and sadness was
Norway, where 10% of adults reported these mental health concerns.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

**Some teachers wrote their own obituaries as part of their
back-to-school prep**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

In addition to all her usual back-to-school preparations this summer,
veteran teacher [Sarah Backstrom wrote her own
obituary](https://cnn.com/2020/08/06/us/iowa-teachers-coronavirus-obits-trnd/index.html)
and sent it to Iowa Governor Kim Reynolds' office. Backstrom said she
was scared about returning to the classroom after Reynolds released
guidance that said at least half of schools' instruction must be
conducted in person -- and that schools couldn't request online-only
education unless their county's positivity rate was 15% or higher.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

She is one of scores of educators across America dreading the start of
the school year. Teachers in Gwinnett County, Georgia are [protesting
the district's
plan](https://cnn.com/2020/08/07/us/georgia-teachers-protest-return-to-school/index.html)
for students to return to school even as hundreds of district employees
have tested positive for Covid-19 or are in quarantine because of
potential exposure. The teachers and their families gathered -- in their
cars -- in Suwanee yesterday, honking horns and displaying signs to
express their displeasure with the district's recent decision to phase
in face-to-face instruction.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

And when a high school student in Dallas, Georgia shared a photo of a
hallway packed with maskless students at her school, [she got
suspended](https://cnn.com/2020/08/07/us/georgia-teen-photo-crowded-school-hallway-trnd/index.html).
"I was concerned for the safety of everyone in that building and
everyone in the county because precautions that the CDC and guidelines
that the CDC has been telling us for months now, weren't being
followed," Hannah Watters said.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

**What's new on the vaccine front**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Australian Prime Minister Scott Morrison urged any nation that
eventually produces a viable [coronavirus vaccine to share
it](https://cnn.com/world/live-news/coronavirus-pandemic-08-07-20-intl/h_ed9071aa4e540f3f320425d2f08a818e)
with the world. "Any country that was to find this vaccine and not make
it available around the world without restraint... will be judged
terribly by history," Morrison said at a news conference today.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

Meanwhile in Brazil, President Jair Bolsonaro said that the experimental
vaccine developed by the University of Oxford and biopharmaceutical
company AstraZeneca may be available to Brazilians from "December or
January" -- and that once that happens, the pandemic "would be overcome"
in a matter of weeks. The vaccine is currently under phase three testing
in Brazil.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

In the US, President Trump said yesterday that he was "optimistic" a
vaccine would be ready around election day on November 3, even as [his
own experts urged
caution](https://cnn.com/2020/08/07/politics/donald-trump-vaccines-election-2020/index.html).

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

As of this week, 26 vaccines are in human trials around the world and a
further 139 are in pre-clinical trials.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

**US lifts 'Do Not Travel' advisory. But where can Americans go?**

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

After more than four months, the US State Department [lifted yesterday
its
advisory](https://cnn.com/2020/08/06/politics/state-department-level-4-advisory-lifted/index.html)
warning US citizens against traveling abroad. The department issued the
Level 4: Do Not Travel advisory -- the highest level -- on March 19,
urging US citizens not to travel overseas due to the coronavirus
pandemic.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

But even though the advisory has been lifted, American travelers
continue to face severe restrictions in countries worldwide due to the
severity of the crisis in the US.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

The [European Union has blocked entry to US
tourists](https://cnn.com/travel/article/us-international-travel-covid-19/index.html),
and the UK requires travelers from the US to quarantine for 14 days.

</div>

<div class="zn-body__paragraph">

There are also restrictions on non-essential travel between the US and
its neighbors to the north and south -- Canada and Mexico -- until at
least late August.

</div>
