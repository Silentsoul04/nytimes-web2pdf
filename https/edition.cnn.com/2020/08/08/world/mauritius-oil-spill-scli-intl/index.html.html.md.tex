\section{A stricken ship is leaking tonnes of fuel into the 'pristine
lagoons' of the Indian
Ocean}\label{a-stricken-ship-is-leaking-tonnes-of-fuel-into-the-pristine-lagoons-of-the-indian-ocean}}

By Sara Spary and Amy Woodyatt, CNN

Updated 1123 GMT (1923 HKT) August 8, 2020

Chat with us in Facebook Messenger. Find out what's happening in the
world as it unfolds.

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200808043143-02-mauritius-oil-spill-large-169.jpg}

Mauritius has declared a state of environmental emergency over the
spill.

(CNN)\href{https://cnn.com/travel/article/mauritius-underwater-waterfall-must-see-attractions/index.html}{Mauritius}
is facing a "state of environmental emergency" after a wrecked ship
began breaking up, spilling oil into the Indian Ocean.

The vessel ran aground at Pointe d'Esny on the east of the island nation
in late July, according to
\href{https://twitter.com/Ecoaustral/status/1291982398742081536?s=20}{local
media reports} and environmental campaigners. This is close to the Blue
Bay Marine Park reserve and a number of popular tourist beaches.

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200807105007-02-mauritius-oil-spill-scli-intl-large-169.jpg}

Photographs of the leak show thick, sticky oils coating the water and
the ground.

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200808043129-01-mauritius-oil-spill-large-169.jpg}

The vessel lies close to Blue Bay Marine Park reserve and a number of
popular tourist beaches.

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200807111224-01-mauritius-ship-oil-leak-0806-large-169.jpg}

Bystanders look on near Blue Bay Marine Park on August 6.

Damage to the boat has since caused it to leak out polluting waste into
the surrounding area, and photographs circulating on social media appear
to show thick, sticky oils coating the water and the ground.

On Friday, Prime Minister Pravind Jugnauth declared a state of
environmental emergency. Mauritius lies next to the French island of
Reunion in the Indian Ocean, and Jugnauth also appealed to French
President Emmanuel Macron for help on Twitter.

The Mauritian leader
\href{https://twitter.com/PKJugnauth/status/1291780830805790721}{said}
"our country does not have the skills and expertise to refloat stranded
ships."

\href{/2020/07/17/middleeast/yemen-oil-tanker-catastrophe-intl/index.html}{}

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200717095347-01-yemen-sfo-safer-tanker-restricted-large-169.jpg}

\href{/2020/07/17/middleeast/yemen-oil-tanker-catastrophe-intl/index.html}{Time
running out to prevent oil spill from 'ticking time bomb' tanker in
Yemen, UN warns}

Read More

On Saturday, Macron said that France was deploying teams and equipment
from Reunion Island in response.

Happy Khambule, Greenpeace Africa senior climate and energy campaign
manager, warned that the bulk carrier was leaking "tonnes of diesel and
oil into the ocean," and threatening the area's wildlife.

"Thousands of species around the pristine lagoons of Blue Bay, Pointe
d'Esny and Mahebourg are at risk of drowning in a sea of pollution, with
dire consequences for Mauritius' economy, food security and health,"
Khambule said in a Friday
\href{https://www.greenpeace.org/africa/en/press/11864/greenpeace-africa-response-to-mauritius-oil-spill/}{statement.}

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200808043151-03-mauritius-oil-spill-large-169.jpg}

Greenpeace warned that the leak threatened thousands of species.

\includegraphics{//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/200807102927-01-mauritius-oil-spill-scli-intl-large-169.jpg}

A clean-up operation is underway.

The country's minister of the environment Kavy Ramano
\href{https://twitter.com/Environment_MU/status/1291753880007651331}{said}
on Friday: "We are in a situation of environmental crisis."

Younous Omarjee, a member of the European Parliament from Reunion
Island, told CNN that international cooperation was needed to deal with
the "ecological disaster."

"This is a serious environmental crisis and we are not yet aware of all
the consequences it could have. It's also terrible for Mauritius, whose
economy is based on tourism and has already been severely impacted by
the pandemic," he told CNN.

France's Minister of Ecological Transition Barbara Pompili
\href{https://twitter.com/barbarapompili/status/1292000835371753472}{announced}
Saturday that France is providing support to the Mauritian authorities
to deal with the fuel leak.

CNN's Martin Goillandeau and Paul P. Murphy contributed reporting.
