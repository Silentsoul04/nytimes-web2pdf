# Dizzy Fae Makes R\&B Music That Defies Labels

</div>

After touring with Lizzo, she released a mixtape and performed during
World Pride.

<div class="css-53u6y8">

**Name:** Dizzy Fae

**Age:** 21

**Hometown:** St. Paul

**Now lives:** In a one-bedroom apartment in South Minneapolis

**Claim to fame:** Ms. Fae makes [genre-defying
R\&B](https://soundcloud.com/dizzy-fae) music that drifts between
experimental electronica, jazz and the sensual grooves of “The Velvet
Rope”-era Janet Jackson. “I create music that is evolving,” she said.
Her uncategorizable sound comes naturally to her as a young biracial
woman who identifies as queer. “I never question sharing my authenticity
with anyone,” she said.

**Big break:** She wrote her debut single, “[Color Me
Bad](https://soundcloud.com/dizzy-fae/color-me-bad),” when she was just
16. When Zane Lowe, the influential BBC Radio 1 D.J.,
[premiered](https://twitter.com/dizzyfae/status/717788243094216705) the
track on his Beats 1 radio show in 2016, Ms. Fae was in the middle of a
senior-year vocal class at the Saint Paul Conservatory For Performing
Artists. “My teachers were worried because I kept running to the
bathroom to check my phone,” she said. Shortly after graduation, she
joined
[Lizzo](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/09/18/style/lizzo-truth-hurts.html)
on her nationwide tour. In 2018, she dropped her debut mixtape “[Free
Form](https://soundcloud.com/dizzy-fae/sets/dizzy-fae-free-form-mixtape).”
“‘Free Form’ is a free-flowing spirit doing whatever she wants and
calling it a mixtape,” she said.

**Latest project:** Her new mixtape, “[No
GMO](https://soundcloud.com/dizzy-fae/sets/no-gmo-mixtape-1),” was
released in June. The project’s title refers to her interest in avoiding
unnecessary additives, whether in the kitchen or recording studio. “It’s
important to stay clean,” she said. “You are what you eat.”

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

**Next thing:** Ms. Fae is putting together a club night in her hometown
called “Dizzyland” and an European tour that will kick off in October.
“I am a very spontaneous person,” she said. “Each day I wake up not
knowing what will happen.”

**Double rainbow:** In celebration of this year’s World Pride in New
York, she performed the headlining set at [Leslie
Pride 2019](https://outbuzz.com/gay-parties/leslie-pride-2019/), a queer
party held in [the cellar of Hotel 50
Bowery](https://www.conceptny.com). “It had been raining that day and I
looked up at the sky and there were two rainbows,” she said. “It was one
of those days I will be talking about for years to come.”
