In the midst of the \#MeToo movement in 2018, Bindu Bansinath wrote,
“[How ‘Lolita’ Freed Me From My Own
Humbert](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/16/style/modern-love-how-lolita-freed-me-from-my-own-humbert.html),”
an essay about the sexual abuse she experienced as a teenager at the
hands of an older man who was close friends with her parents.

When she was 15, Ms. Bansinath read Vladimir Nabokov’s “Lolita” in an
attempt to normalize her situation. Instead, the book provided a road
map for an escape.

This week on the Modern Love podcast, [Jameela
Jamil](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/09/24/style/jameela-jamil-does-her-own-makeup-sometimes-in-a-car.html)
reads Ms. Bansinath’s essay. Ms. Jamil is a British actress known for
her role as Tahani Al-Jamil on “[The Good
Place](https://www.nbc.com/the-good-place).” She is the host of the
podcast “[I
Weigh](https://www.earwolf.com/show/i-weigh-with-jameela-jamil/),” which
challenges accepted ideas about body image and weight.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

Ms. Bansinath is a [writer](https://www.bindubansinath.com/) in New York
City, working on her first novel. She is pursuing an M.F.A. in writing
at Columbia University. Stay tuned after the reading to hear more from
her, Ms. Jamil and the Modern Love editor Daniel Jones.
