# How to Make a Dish Towel Tote Bag, With Rodarte

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

Laura and Kate Mulleavy show us how to turn kitchen towels and costume
jewelry into a carry-all.

<div class="css-1wlr991">

<div class="css-18e8msd">

<div class="css-1lhhykl epjyd6m0">

<div class="css-1baulvz">

By [<span class="css-1baulvz last-byline" itemprop="name">Vanessa
Friedman</span>](https://www.nytimes3xbfgragh.onion/by/vanessa-friedman)

Illustrations by
<span class="css-1baulvz last-byline" itemprop="name">Samantha
Hahn</span>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

June 2, 2020

Dish towels have never seemed so important or ubiquitous as they have
during these weeks of lockdown. But they are even more multipurpose than
you might expect, or so say Kate and Laura Mulleavy of Rodarte. The
sisters are known for their imagination and ability to find beauty in
unexpected places.

“We are all stuck at home, but even if you are just hauling laundry from
one room to another, it makes you feel better to be able to put stuff
away in something that cheers you up,” Laura Mulleavy said. “We have so
many dish towels around and are always in need of reusable totes that we
can also use as storage.”

So they decided to make one out of a dish towel and some costume
jewelry. Here’s how you can,
too.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

**Your tool kit:**

  - One cotton or linen dish towel, approximately 20 by 27 inches.
    Ideally striped (it will make the project easier), but any pattern
    will do.

  - Two costume jewelry necklaces, like the plastic beaded kind that are
    handed out at Mardi Gras, or one chain-link plastic necklace.

  - One thick ribbon

  - Sewing machine with cotton-poly thread or, if you are sewing by
    hand, a tapestry needle and embroidery thread.

  - Straightedge
ruler

  - Scissors

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:245.5333333333333px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 1

Fold your chosen dish towel in half so that the inside is on the
outside.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:653.4666666666666px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 2

Create a bottom. At the fold, measure two inches on each side,
vertically. Mark and draw a line from one dot to the other. Then flatten
the lower part of the folded towel so that it makes a T-shape
perpendicular to the body, four inches across. This will become the
“bottom” of the
tote.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:346.0666666666667px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 3

Sew each side of the flattened part together with a straight stitch half
an inch from each edge so that the sides of the T are closed. This will
form a V-shaped notch at the bottom of the towel. If looked at from the
side, it will resemble a
Y.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:174.64444444444442px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 4

Sew each side together with a straight stitch, vertically from the
bottom of the V to the upper end of the folded towel. Invert the bag and
press the bottom open to make a
floor.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:228.13333333333335px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 5

Make the sides. Measure a quarter of an inch from each side seam and
straight stitch vertically from the top around the bottom and up the
other side to create a soft rectangular side that will turn the sac into
a box
shape.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:238.44444444444443px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 6

Fold each necklace in
half.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:402.1333333333333px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 7

Cut a ribbon in four pieces, each piece four inches long. Loop each
ribbon through each of the four ends in the two lengths of necklace,
bringing the ends of the ribbon
together.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:390.53333333333336px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 8

Measure to the center of the upper, open rim of the bag. Then measure
three and a half inches from there in either direction and mark those
spots. That is where the loops, or the two ends of the handles, will
go.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:434.35555555555555px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 9

Secure the loops to the bag at two stitch points, the upper one
one-eighth of an inch from the top edge of the bag, the lower one
one-eighth of an inch from the bottom of the loop
ribbon.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-79elbk" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper">

<div class="css-z3e15g" data-testid="photoviewer-wrapper-hidden">

</div>

<div class="css-1a48zt4 ehw59r15" data-testid="photoviewer-children">

<div class="css-1xdhyk6 erfvjey0">

<span class="css-1ly73wi e1tej78p0">Image</span>

<div class="css-zjzyr8">

<div data-testid="lazyimage-container" style="height:548.4222222222223px">

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

STEP 10

Put bag over your arm and go\!

</div>

</div>
