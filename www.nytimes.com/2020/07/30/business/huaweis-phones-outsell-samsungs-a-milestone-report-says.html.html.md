<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

Strong sales in China helped **Huawei** edge out **Samsung** to become
the world’s biggest smartphone vendor in the second quarter, according
to [the research firm
Canalys](https://www.canalys.com/newsroom/Canalys-huawei-samsung-worldwide-smartphone-market-q2-2020)
— a milestone for the embattled Chinese tech giant.

Distrust of Huawei’s products is rising all around the world. [The Trump
administration](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/05/15/business/economy/commerce-department-huawei.html),
concerned that the Chinese government might use Huawei gear to disrupt
telecommunications networks, has restricted the company’s access to
American technology.

And [Britain this
month](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/07/14/business/huawei-uk-5g.html)said
it would bar Huawei equipment from the country’s 5G mobile networks.
Other governments have mulled similar moves.

Huawei has doubled down on the Chinese market in response, a strategy
that has paid off during the pandemic. China’s economy is recovering
more quickly than others, helping Huawei sell 55.8 million handsets
between April and June, according to Canalys. Samsung sold 53.7 million
devices.

</div>

</div>

<div class="css-1fanzo5 StoryBodyCompanionColumn">

<div class="css-53u6y8">

Samsung has for years been the world’s largest smartphone seller. But
its major markets, including the United States, Europe and Brazil, are
deeper in the coronavirus-related economic doldrums.

“This is a remarkable result that few people would have predicted a year
ago,” said Ben Stanton, a Canalys analyst. “If it wasn’t for Covid-19,
it wouldn’t have happened.”
