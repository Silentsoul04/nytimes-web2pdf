# 11 of Our Best Weekend Reads

</div>

The kindness of strangers in Beirut. Students share what it’s like to
return to school. 75 years since the atomic bombings. Burna Boy’s
Afro-fusion music. And more.

<div class="css-18e8msd">

<div class="css-vp77d3 epjyd6m0">

Welcome to the weekend. I found this week sobering. Did you feel the
same? If your heart broke seeing the devastation in Beirut, you might
want to take a look at [how you can
help](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/05/world/how-to-help-lebanon-beirut.html).
Maybe you just need these [quick stress-reducing
strategies](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/06/well/mind/five-minute-coronavirus-stress-resets.html)
— I certainly do. Wherever you are, I hope that you are safe and that
the weekend brings you a chance to rest and recharge. Take care and
enjoy some incredible journalism.

## [I was bloodied and dazed. Beirut strangers treated me like a friend.](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/04/world/middleeast/lebanon-explosion-beirut.html)

Vivian Yee, The New York Times’s Middle East correspondent, was hurt in
the explosions in Beirut. In this [first-person account of what
happened](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/04/world/middleeast/lebanon-explosion-beirut.html),
she describes how people knew what to do, including helping wounded
people they didn’t know. You can also [hear her on The Times’s podcast
“The
Daily.”](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/06/podcasts/the-daily/beirut-explosion-damage.html)
