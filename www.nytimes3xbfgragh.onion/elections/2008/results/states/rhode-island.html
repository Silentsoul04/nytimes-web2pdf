State Highlights

Rhode Island is an overwhelmingly blue state, only slightly less Democratic than neighboring Massachusetts, and it demonstrated that again in this election.

Senator Barack Obama easily won the state’s four electoral votes.

Senator Jack Reed, a Democrat seeking a third term, overwhelmed his Republican opponent, Robert G. Tingle, a casino pit boss whom Mr. Reed defeated in 2002.

As a member of the Senate Committee on Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs, Mr. Reed in 2006 urged Fannie Mae executives to do more to help middle- and low-income home buyers. This fall, he played a role in fashioning the $700 billion Wall Street rescue package, and people in financial industries helped finance his campaign, on which he spent more than $3.3 million. Mr. Tingle estimated last month that he had spent just $25 of his own money.

The state’s two Democratic representatives also won re-election. In the district that includes the eastern half of the state, Patrick J. Kennedy, a seven-term congressman who is Senator Edward M. Kennedy’s youngest son, defeated Jonathan Scott, a former children’s social service counselor.

In western Rhode Island, Representative Jim Langevin beat Mark S. Zaccaria, the Republican candidate, a former town councilman from North Kingston. SAM DILLON

CORRECTION, NOVEMBER 13, 2008:

An earlier version of this article suggested incorrectly that there was a cause-and-effect relationship between a statement by Senator Jack Reed of Rhode Island, a Democrat who was re-elected to a third term, and the financial crisis at the government-sponsored mortgage finance company Fannie Mae. Senator Reed, a long-time advocate of affordable housing, urged Fannie Mae in 2006 to do more to help middle- and low-income home buyers; the bank’s decisions to invest in risky subprime mortgages, rather than Senator Reed’s urging, led to the Fannie Mae crisis.
