State Highlights

Rhode Island elected its first independent governor, Lincoln D. Chafee, who won despite a late surge for the Republican candidate, John Robitaille.

Economic issues dominated the campaign in a three-way race, with the candidates differing on whether new taxes, or tax cuts, would help most. But a nonendorsement by President Obama late last month played an important role in shaping the outcome of the race.

Mr. Obama withheld his support from the Democratic candidate, Frank T. Caprio as a favor to Mr. Chafee, who had endorsed him in 2008. Mr. Caprio announced that the president could “take his endorsement and really shove it.” The remark, in a state where Mr. Obama’s popularity is still relatively high, led to a drop in Mr. Caprio’s support. The main beneficiary was Mr. Robitaille, an aide to Gov. Donald L. Carcieri, who could not run again because of term limits.

Democrats held onto both of the state’s House seats.

The smallest state in the union retained its full name, the longest in the union. Voters soundly rejected an amendment to the state constitution that would have shortened the name to “State of Rhode Island” from “State of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations.”
