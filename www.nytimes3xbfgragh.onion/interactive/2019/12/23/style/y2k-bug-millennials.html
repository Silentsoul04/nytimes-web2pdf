Y2K @ 20

By Aliza Aufrichtig, John Herrman, Nellie Bowles, Jonah Engel Bromwich, Taylor Lorenz, Rachel Monroe, Rumsey Taylor, Tracy Ma, Choire Sicha and Joanna NikasDec. 23, 2019

Anxiety about the millennium1 created2 millennial anxiety3. There was a problem with the computers 4. Or was there5? The people6 who warned7 us8 were wrong. Or were they9? They told us the elevators10 would11 crash12 and the planes13 would fall straight out of the sky. Whatever money14 your parents15 had saved16 would be deleted when the bank17 accounts18 were erased. Hackers would exploit19 the chaos20. Viruses would shut down the few computers21 that remained. Slot machines would barf22 up all their coins — all because computers23 were only required to record the last two digits of the year. That New24 Year’s25 Eve26, no one knew if the clocks27 would click over from 1999 to 200028 … or to 190029. Billions of dollars30 were spent31 to save32 our future. This was 20 years ago now, yet it marked a generation of young people forever33.
