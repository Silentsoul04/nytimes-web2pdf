    They Wanted Some Private Outdoor Space in Williamsburg. But Where Is the Best Place to Find It?

    By JOYCE COHEN SEPT. 3, 2020

    For their first purchase together, a couple searched their old Brooklyn neighborhood for a little fresh air — and maybe a second bedroom. Which of these options would you choose? Related Article

    In a quintessential New York arrangement, Patrick Galvin and Praveen Maddela lived for several years in a Williamsburg, Brooklyn, rental with another couple.

    The four friends shared a triplex, with a terrace and rooftop access, found on Craigslist. The rent of around $3,500 a month was more than reasonable, and the living situation harmonious.

    “It totally worked,” Mr. Galvin, 33, said.

    Besides, nobody was home all that much. “We, like many New Yorkers, worked a ton,” he said, “and were always going out to opera and theater.”

    Still, interest rates were falling, and after six years together, Mr. Galvin, who is from Minnesota and works in development at the Metropolitan Opera, and Mr. Maddela, who grew up on Staten Island and works in financial technology, were ready to buy a place of their own.

    “I am going to be close to 40 soon,” said Mr. Maddela, 38. “I can’t be living with two other adults. We can’t tell people, ‘Yeah, we live with another couple.’ We aren’t in college. We are all professionals.”

    Last fall, they set about looking for a place in a small, relatively new condominium building with plenty of private outdoor space. A multilevel layout appealed to them, as they enjoyed the separation of space created by the staircases in their rental. They hoped to stay within a few blocks of the rental, which was near the Lorimer Street stop on the L train — within walking distance of the hustle-bustle of Williamsburg, but not in the thick of it.

    They were prepared to spend up to $1 million for a place with monthly fees of no more than $1,000, and figured they could buy a one-bedroom, although they were hoping for a second bedroom or some kind of bonus space.

    The couple contacted a friend, Nick Hovsepian, a licensed salesman at the Corcoran Group.

    “It didn’t need to be large, but they wanted large outdoor space,” Mr. Hovsepian said. “They understood that what they were looking for was rare.”

    Among their choices:

    No. 1

    Devoe Street Duplex

    This 750-square-foot, one-bedroom duplex had two outdoor spaces — a terrace off the living room on the lower level and another off the room above — plus a shared roof deck. The asking price was $950,000, with monthly charges of around $650.

    No. 2

    Grand Street Duplex

    This one-bedroom duplex was around 740 square feet, with an extra bathroom and three private outdoor spaces, including two roof terraces upstairs, one on either side. It was steps from the Brooklyn-Queens Expressway. The price was $819,000, with monthly charges of a little more than $300.

    No. 3

    Lorimer Street Two-Bedroom

    This two-bedroom was about 660 square feet, with a front terrace off the living room and a rear terrace off the bedrooms. There was a washer-dryer hookup, but no machines, unlike in the other two options. The price was $900,000, with monthly charges in the mid $500s.
