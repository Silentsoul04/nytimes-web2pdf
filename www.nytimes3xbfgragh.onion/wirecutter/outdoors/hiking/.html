Hiking

  1. Outdoors
  2. Hiking
Even a simple hike can throw several curve balls your way, from changing weather to varying terrain. Being too cold, too hot, or otherwise uncomfortable while on the trail is no way to enjoy the outdoors. We’ve sifted through all the research and done all the testing to come up with the best gear to keep you hiking comfortably, from hiking boots to rain jackets.

  • The Best Thermal Underwear for Women and Men

    Updated September 3, 2020

    by Eve O'Neill and Liz Thomas

    We sent 13 testers out to hike, ski, shovel snow, and run, and we found base layers in a variety of styles and fabrics that will keep all types of bodies warm.

  • The Best Hiking Boots

    Updated June 25, 2020

    by Ebony Roberts and Jenni Gritters

    After hiking 950 miles in 36 pairs of hiking boots, we’ve decided the Salomon Quest 4D 3 GTX Hiking Boots (men’s and women’s) are the ones we want on our feet.

  • The Best Sun Hat for Hikers

    Updated June 9, 2020

    by Liz Thomas

    Out testers hiked 200 hours to find an effective sun hat for outdoor activities. The Sunday Afternoons Ultra Adventure Hat is their unanimous favorite, providing maximum protection for head, neck, and face.

  • The Best Hydration Packs for Day Hikers

    Updated May 19, 2020

    by James Meigs and Jennifer Stern

    After spending more than 50 hours researching and testing a dozen hydration-system daypacks, we think that the Osprey Skarab 18 and Osprey Skimmer 16 are best for most men and women planning a half-day hike.

  • The Best Nonslip Traction Devices

    Updated May 11, 2020

    by Lisa Maloney

    After slipping and sliding on trails and streets around Anchorage, Alaska, here are the best ways to stay upright, whether walking, hiking, or running.

  • The Best Trekking Poles

    Updated May 7, 2020

    by Ryan Bradley

    After 40 hours of testing on trails, streets, beaches, and more, we’re determined that the Montem Ultra Strong Trekking Poles are the best for nearly everyone.

  • The Best Hiking Socks

    Updated May 7, 2020

    by Ebony Roberts

    After hiking 160 miles in 18 pairs of hiking socks, we chose the men’s and women’s Darn Tough Light Hiker Micro Crew as the best hiking socks for most people.

  • The Best First Aid Kit for Hiking and the Outdoors

    Updated April 24, 2020

    by Anna Perling

    After 14 hours of research and tests of 12 wilderness first aid kits, we think Adventure Medical’s Mountain Backpacker Kit is best for most weekend warriors.

  • The Best Winter Boots

    Updated March 19, 2020

    by Eve O'Neill

    After trudging through snow and ice in 29 pairs of boots, we’ve chosen nine that will keep you warm, dry, and stable all winter long.

  • The Best Hiking Baby Carriers

    Updated November 29, 2019

    by Caitlin Giddings

    After hiking almost 100 miles in 10 different baby carriers, we’ve found that the Deuter Kid Comfort is the carrier we’d choose for our kids (and our backs).

  • The Best Hand Warmer

    Updated November 18, 2019

    by Kaitlyn Wells

    After testing nine new hand warmers we think the Human Creations EnergyFlux Enduro is the best way to keep your hands warm this winter.

    Published August 26, 2016

    by Kalee Thompson

    After evaluating a dozen popular packs, we chose six and kid-tested them in the wilds of suburban Los Angeles (and the surrounding hills and trails). We picked two that will work for most families, both from REI: the Tarn 12 for little kids and the Tarn 18 for bigger kids.
